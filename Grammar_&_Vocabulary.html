English Grammar:-

Alphabet vs Alphabets:

Alphabet means the total number of letters which are in that language(i.e In english letters A-Z total 26), while Alphabets means languages(i.e German,Spanish or French)itself.

Word:

A word is a sequence of letters arranged such a way without any space among them and clearly giving specific meaning.For example house,man,woman etc.If we take first word house and arrange the letters as useoh, this will not give any specific meaning thus it is not a word.

Phrase:

A phrase is a sequence of words without subject-verb which is meaninful but does not give complete information.It is the part of clause,further cluase is the part of sentence.For exmple before driving,honestly telling etc.

Clause:

A clause is a sequence of words which is meaninful & contains at leat one subject & one verb.cluase or clauses is/are the part of sentence.A sentence may contain one or more clauses.For example- Ram was here when I came.In this sentence there are two clauses i.e 1.Ram(S) was(V) here. 2.I(S) came(V).

Clauses are divided into two categories

1.Independent or main clause:The clause which can withstand in the sentence without the help of any other clause i.e implies complete meaning , is called independent or main clause. For example in the previous sentence Ram was here when I came .Here 1st clause(Ram was here) is independent because it implies complete meaning but 2nd clause(when I came) is dependent or subordinate clause because it requires some other information to complete it.

2.Dependent or Subordinate clause:Dependent clause depends on other one or more independent or dependent clauses in the sentence to imply the complete sense.For example in the previous sentence Ram was here when I came .Here 1st clause(Ram was here) is independent because it implies complete meaning but 2nd clause(when I came) is dependent or subordinate clause because it requires some other information to complete it.

Parts of speech:

1.Noun:

Noun is visible or invisible things :

Visible things: Names of Person,place or things.

invisible things : Felt only: FAQS:

F:Feelings..Love,Happiness,joy,kindness etc

A:Actions..Arrival,motion,still etc

Q:Quality..Honesty,Love,hard work,integrity,intelligence etc

S:State..Benefit,harm etc

How to check whether it's noun or not:

Ask questions Who,Whom,What in the sentence & the answer we get, will be the noun. For example:In the sentence Ram is a cricket player. If we ask question that who is cricket player? we will get the answer ....Ram.So here Ram(A name of a person) is a noun.

Let's take another example Ganga is a holy river. If we ask the question that what is holy river? we will get the answer Ganga.Which is the name of thing & hence is a noun.

Let's take another example-Ramesh gave Seema an English Grammar book.Here if we ask who gave an English Grammar Book? we will get answer-Ramesh, which is the name of a person & hence is a noun.Further If we ask question that Ramesh gave an English Grammar Book to whom, we will get the answer Seema which is again the name of a person & hence is a noun.Further If we ask question that Ramesh gave Seema what?, we will get the answer an English Grammar Book which is the name of a thing & hence is a noun.

Types of Noun:

(I)Countable noun: Noun which can be counted i.e pens,books,colleges etc.

(II)Unountable noun: Noun which can not be counted i.e oil,honesty,motion,arrival etc.

Now Countable nouns are further divided into three categories:

(i)Proper: For example Ram,Ganga,Philips(any brand name) etc.

(ii)Common: For example Person,boy,Animal,insects,birds,etc.

(ii)Collective: A collection of things For example band of armed men,bunch of grapes,Class,Family,bevy,gang etc.

Now Uncountable nouns are further divided into two categories:

(i)Material: It covers material things For example Milk,Diesal,Petrol,Water,Iron etc.

(ii)Abstract: FAQS: For example Happiness,sadness,joy,kindness,cruelity,grief,.Arrival,dparture,motion,still, Honesty,Love,hard work,integrity,intelligence etc.
